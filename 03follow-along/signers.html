Who Were the Signers

Famous Signers

Some of the most famous signers of the Decleration of Independance include:

Thomas Jefferson
Principal author of the Declaration of Independence and later the third U.S. President.
John Adams
A strong advocate for independence who later became the second U.S. President.
Benjamin Franklin
Renowned diplomat, inventor, and writer; the oldest signer at age 70.
John Hancock
President of the Continental Congress, famous for his bold signature.
Samuel Adams
Influential leader in the push toward independence and organizer of the Boston Tea Party.
Richard Henry Lee
Introduced the resolution calling for the colonies to declare independence.
Roger Sherman
The only person to sign all four major U.S. founding documents.
Charles Carroll of Carrollton
The only Catholic signer and one of the wealthiest individuals in the colonies.

Each of these individuals played a significant role in the founding of the United States and are remembered for their courage in signing the Decleration, an act considered treasonous by the British Crown.
